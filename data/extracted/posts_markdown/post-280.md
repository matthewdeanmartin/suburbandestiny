title: 'Icelandic: Why learn it?'
---


**Something to read.** It has a popular literature– the sagas.  


**Cool sounding.**  Lots of vowels and a few exotic consonants like “ll”


**Tiny discount on learning for English speakers.** A few, often well hidden, cognates.


**Materials available online.** You can visit/lurk the Icelandic speaking section of Eve Online.


**Why not?**  Few speakers globally, and those that you are likely to meet in a tourist setting are likely to already speak English.  It is a natural language, expect a significant learning curve.


**How?**


[Books](http://odin.bio.miami.edu/norse/icelandic.html).


[Online dictionary](http://www.ordabok.is/).


[Travel](http://www.affordable.is/page.php?language=english&version=iceland&id=84). Get out of the city, increase the odds that you will desperately need to speak Icelandic.


Audio media. There are Icelandic podcasts (hlaðvarp) [here](http://www.podcastingnews.com/forum/link_187.htm?sid=e4240eb76d768754363f87080b674359), and [search for podcasts here](http://www.google.is/search?hl=is&q=Hla%C3%B0varp&btnG=Google+leit&lr=).


Just think– all this media over the internet– a continuous stream, free.  It’s almost like immersion language learning if you can avoid checking boingboing and the email.
