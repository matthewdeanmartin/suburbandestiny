I’m thinking of reading up on this language to figure out how it ticks, but not following through to learn it. It takes more than a dictionary to learn a language– in particular you need a community. I’m in the DC area, so I’d have to track down what tiny number of Chamorro speakers there are in DC. By my rough estimates, most Chamorro speakers in North America are in Washington state. In DC, I’d have to be build a community out of people who don’t speak the language as a first language, which means finding a group of incredibly motivated and linguistically talented people. Outside of Guam, typical cultural events would be book, movie, and potluck events– which works well if the language in question has a good set of books in translation, movies with subtitles. 


I found sources of Guamian music, esp [Betelnut Radio](http://betelnutradio.com/), but not so much in the way of books and movies. **Update!** I found the independent movie [Shiro’s Head](http://www.shirosheadthelegend.com/), an authentic Guam/Chamorro movie that is English\-Chamorro with subtitles. [And another Chamorro indie film on the way.](http://www.myspace.com/guamsfirstfeature) A few more movies are not really Chamorro movies: Noon Sunday, Max Havoc, Godzilla, No Man an Island (the last has the Chamorro speaking Tagalog!, the first three just take place/were filmed in Guam.)


As for me personally, as a vegetarian, at a Chamorro potluck I’m too picky to eat most Chamorro authentic foods– a problem I have at Icelandic potlucks as well–and Russian for that matter. This isn’t to say things are too hard for someone else to pull it off, but probably too hard for me to pull it off, especially with so many additional great language out there to study.


**Resources for learners**  

There is a [dictionary](http://www.chamoru.info/dictionary/), it’s online and suitable for converting to Anki flash cards (with some processing).  

There is a [grammar](http://www.amazon.com/Chamorro-Language-William-Edwin-Safford/dp/B002K2RQCQ/ref=sr_1_3?ie=UTF8&qid=1329254956&sr=8-3). ([and others](http://www.amazon.com/Chamorro-Reference-Grammar-Micronesia-Topping/dp/0824802691/ref=sr_1_12?ie=UTF8&qid=1329254956&sr=8-12))  

There is a [text book](http://www.amazon.com/Spoken-Chamorro-Intensive-Language-Grammatical/dp/0870228056/ref=wl_it_dp_o_npd?ie=UTF8&coliid=I2DLFWOENV0FOL&colid=32B22XLLRTLNE).  

There is an [online version of the bible](http://chamorrobible.org/), which appears to be the largest online corpora of Chammoro.  

There is sort\-of a [Chamorro wikipedia](http://ch.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fanhaluman).


There isn’t an online forum or mailing list that I could find.  

There is a [Facebook page](http://www.facebook.com/pages/Chamorro-Language/37561383808).


There are cook books. (Cookbooks? In every language group I’ve been in, there is a significant subgroup who is there just for the food) I have no idea how I’m going to make vegan spam balls.


I couldn’t find any Chamorro comics– another language learning tool I found invaluable when learning Icelandic.


There are youtube videos, and [Betelnut Radio](https://betelnutradio.com/), but no obvious publishing of Chamorro language books, movies, music or other media. 


To find more, google “[Fino’ Chamoru](https://www.google.com/search?as_q=Fino'%20Chamoru)”
