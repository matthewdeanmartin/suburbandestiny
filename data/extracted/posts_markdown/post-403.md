Learning a language is all about productivity.  Paper dictionaries, are not a productive use of time.  What is best is read on line with a online dictionary close enough to allow for cut and paste.


**Online Dictionaries**  

[Dicts.info](http://www.dicts.info/dictionary.php?k1=1&k2=45) : Enter first few letters of word, does an English and Icelandic lookup at the same time.


[University of Wisconsin](http://digicoll.library.wisc.edu/IcelOnline/Search.TEId.html): Enter word, even the inflected form, and UWDC will usually find it.  You can use d for ð. Doesn’t do as good a job with a “starts with” search.


[Google Images](http://images.google.is/imghp?hl=is&tab=gi).  Enter word, search. Example, [Ísbjörn](http://images.google.is/images?hl=is&q=isbjorn&btnG=Leita+a%C3%B0+myndum&gbv=2). Do not search for “að keyra” “to drive” using the google image search technique.


**Motivation**Being motivated about learning a language is all about real social relationships.


Meetup.com Icelandic meetups, like the [DC Icelandic Meetup](http://icelandic.meetup.com/1/).  This has been the best thing for my Icelandic skills outside of BYKI vocabulary drills.


[Italki](http://www.italki.com/), online social networking for language learners.  I personally haven’t benefited from this service, yet, but it shows promise.
